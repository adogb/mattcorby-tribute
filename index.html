About Matt Corby

At the origin

Corby was raised in Oyster Bay, a suburban enclave of Sydney's Sutherland Shire. His first musical memory is being driven past a guitar shop as a five year old and being captivated by the rows of shiny instruments in the window. His dad, John, bought his son a three-quarter acoustic guitar, on which Corby started having one lesson a week. The first song he learned to play was Greensleeves, but his dedication began to wane and singing soon took priority. At age 14 he left school, joined a Christian voca group called Iron and Clay and hit the road. During that time he met 19-year-old Jarryd James.

A big mess

Corby later left the group and returned to school. In 2007 he participated to the show Australian Idol. Though Corby finished second, for him the whole experience would turn out to be "a big mess". Corby describes the year after Idol as one of the most difficult of his life. Corby felt he had so much to prove. The five EPs he released before his new album Telluric were born out of his desperation to prove he was more than an ex-Idol but also as a therapy.

Recognition at last

In 2011 his song Brother went on to win an ARIA for Song of the Year, and Resolution earned the same award a year later.

Creating Telluric

Corby came back to Sydney from Los Angeles after scrapping a full-length recording of the album. For two years he set to "learn a bunch of musical instruments" to be able to create this album exactly like he envisionned it in his mind. Telluric was released in March 2016.
